Just minutes from **Rabat–Salé Airport** and major transport hubs, the Palais des Congrès ensures convenience, comfort, and an unforgettable experience.

## Transportation

### Recommended Transportation Options

**Blassty**
Blassty is the first multimodal mobility application in Morocco, launched by Rabat Région Mobilité (RRM). It unifies urban transportation across Rabat, Salé, and Témara, providing real-time bus and tram schedules, parking availability, and optimized route planning.

* **Trains (ONCF):** Efficient for traveling between Rabat and nearby cities like Casablanca, Kenitra, Tangier, and Marrakech.
* **Trams (Rabat-Salé Tramway):** Modern tram network connecting major districts, ideal for daily commuting.
* **Petit Taxi (Blue in Rabat):** Small, affordable taxis for short trips within the city.
    * *Note:* They cannot travel between cities (e.g., Rabat to Salé). Always check the meter.
* **Grand Taxi (White):** Larger taxis for longer distances or shared group travel. They follow predefined routes and can travel between cities.
* **Buses:** Inexpensive public transport covering most areas, though potentially slower due to traffic.
* **Walking:** Central areas and medinas are pedestrian-friendly.

### From Airport/Station to Venue

**From Rabat–Salé Airport**
* **Grand Taxi / Private Transfer:** Recommended direct ride (15–20 minutes).
* **Car Rental:** Available at the airport.

**From Rabat Ville Train Station**
* **Grand Taxi / Private Transfer:** 10–15 minute ride.
* **Tram + Taxi:** Take the tram within the city and switch to a Petit Taxi for the final leg.

### Public Transportation Etiquette
* Allow passengers to exit before boarding.
* Offer seats to elderly passengers, pregnant women, and people with disabilities.
* Keep your voice low and avoid loud phone conversations.
* Validate your ticket before boarding.

## About Rabat

![Rabat Cityscape](/assets/images/rabat-city.jpg)

Rabat is the capital city of Morocco and one of the country’s four imperial cities. Located on the Atlantic coast at the mouth of the Bouregreg River, it faces the city of Salé. Known for its safety, cleanliness, and green spaces, Rabat is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that combines rich history with modern infrastructure.

Key landmarks include the **Hassan Tower**, the **Mausoleum of Mohammed V**, and the **Kasbah of the Udayas**.

### Language
* **Official:** Arabic (local dialect: *Darija*) and Amazigh (Berber).
* **Business/Education:** French is widely used.
* **International:** English is increasingly spoken, especially in tourism and conferences. Spanish is heard in some regions.

### Weather
Rabat enjoys a mild Mediterranean climate.
* **March Weather:** This marks the transition to spring.
* **Temperatures:** Daytime averages **16°C – 20°C**, with cooler evenings.
* **Rainfall:** Decreases compared to winter; sunny days are frequent.

### Currency
* **Currency:** Moroccan Dirham (MAD).
* **Cash:** Recommended for small purchases, taxis, and markets. ATMs are widely available.
* **Cards:** Accepted in hotels and larger shops.
* *Note:* The Dirham is a closed currency and cannot be taken out of the country.

### Power Plugs/Outlets
* **Type:** Type C and Type E (European standard).
* **Voltage:** 220V / 50Hz.
* *Recommendation:* Bring a universal adapter if you are from the UK, USA, or Asia.

### Clothing
* **Style:** Modern and relaxed. Smart casual is suitable for the conference.
* **March:** Layering is key due to warm days and cooler nights.
* **Shoes:** Comfortable walking shoes are essential for exploring the Medina.

### Water
Tap water is generally treated, but **bottled water** is recommended for visitors for taste and safety. It is affordable and widely available.

## Local Customs & Etiquette

### Do's
* Greet people politely; handshakes are common.
* Use your **right hand** when giving or receiving items.
* Respect personal space.
* Learn basic phrases like *Salam Alaykum* (Hello) and *Shukran* (Thank you).

### Don'ts
* Avoid public displays of affection.
* Do not take photos of people without permission.
* Avoid discussing sensitive political or religious topics.
* Do not eat or drink on public transport.

### Tipping
Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory.
* **Cafés/Restaurants:** 5 to 20 MAD (or round up the bill).
* **Note:** The standard 10-15% rule seen elsewhere is not strictly followed here.

## Things to Do in Rabat

* **The Medina:** Traditional markets for handicrafts and carpets.
* **Bouregreg Marina:** Scenic boat views and cafés.
* **Kasbah of the Udayas:** Historic fortress with Andalusian gardens.
* **Hassan Tower & Mausoleum of Mohammed V:** Iconic historical sites.
* **Chellah Necropolis:** Roman and medieval ruins with beautiful gardens.
* **Mohammed VI Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art:** For art lovers.
